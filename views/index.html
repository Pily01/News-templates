Ammonia: the energy of tomorrow

We are committed to a sustainable future.

Carbon-Free System

A clean-burning fuel: a significant advantage over coal and oil.

Powerful

It can be used to power a wide variety of devices, which produce fewer emissions.

Portable

It can be easily stored and transported, which reduces the cost, and space requirements.

The potential to be a major energy resource

Ammonia can be used as fuel for internal combustion engines, gas turbines, power cars, and generate electricity.

Ammonia: The Clean Energy
That Drives Innovation

Ammonia is a versatile and environmentally friendly energy source. It can be produced from renewable resources such as biomass. This process involves breaking down organic matter such as wood or agricultural waste into hydrogen and carbon monoxide.

A solution to a worldwide problem

The planet's average surface temperature has risen about 2 degrees Fahrenheit (1 degrees Celsius) since the late 19th century, a change driven largely by increased carbon dioxide emissions.

Ammonia: The Power to Change
a Dark Future

Ammonia has several advantages as an energy source. It is inexpensive to produce, clean, renewable, and its combustion releases a relatively small amount of carbon dioxide. In addition, ammonia can be used in fuel cells to generate electricity with high efficiency.
